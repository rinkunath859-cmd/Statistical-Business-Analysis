HYPOTHESIS TEST RESULTS (DATA-BASED OUTPUT)

--------------------------------------------------
Test 1: Normality Test (Shapiro-Wilk)
--------------------------------------------------
H0: The data is normally distributed
H1: The data is not normally distributed

Test Statistic = 0.73 (approx)
p-value = 0.00000126

Decision:
Since p-value < 0.05, reject H0

Conclusion:
The Total_Sales data is NOT normally distributed.


--------------------------------------------------
Test 2: One-Sample T-Test
--------------------------------------------------
H0: Mean of Total_Sales is equal to benchmark
H1: Mean is different

Test Statistic = 0.00
p-value = 1.0000

Decision:
Fail to reject H0

Conclusion:
No significant difference found.
(Note: Benchmark equals sample mean, so result is expected)


--------------------------------------------------
Test 3: Regression Analysis Significance
--------------------------------------------------
Model: Total_Sales ~ Quantity

R-squared = 0.4735

Conclusion:
The model explains 47.35% of variation in Total_Sales.
This indicates a moderate relationship between Quantity and Sales.
